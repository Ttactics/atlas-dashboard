Hygiene-only — the conversion opportunity

59 patients came in for hygiene this month and left with nothing else scheduled. Even converting 20 of those into one restorative procedure at $300–400 adds $6,000–8,000 to the month. This is the single most actionable lever available right now. Collections note: a new billing team has been brought on to address the 71.6% collections rate — this is the right move and will significantly impact cash flow once stabilized.

The three levers that move the number
1. Treatment acceptance rate
If you're presenting $150K in treatment and accepting 55%, you're collecting $82K. Get to 65% and you're at $97K without changing anything else. This is the single fastest path to $100K.
2. Hygiene reappointment and recare retention
With 75–90 new patients per month for nearly a year, you're building a significant active base. If those patients aren't returning, hygiene is leaking. Pre-schedule before checkout. Target 90%+ reappointment.
3. Elective and high-value service mix
Clear aligners, implants, veneers, night guards, and whitening are cash-pay or low-insurance-dependency services. Even 5–8 elective cases per month meaningfully shifts the production number and the patient mix toward your 45–65 target demographic.

Daily CEO checklist

Dr. Mohammad — 10 minutes every morning before patients begin. Resets each session.

Morning huddle prep — 5 min
Review today's schedule for production target
Is today on track to hit the daily production goal? Identify any gaps or underbooked slots before the day starts.
Flag patients with open treatment plans on today's schedule
Who has unscheduled or pending treatment? Know before they sit in the chair so you can have the conversation naturally, not reactively.
Review any new patient consultations scheduled today
Know their intake info. Be ready to present comprehensive treatment — not just respond to their chief complaint. Every new patient is a long-term relationship, not a single visit.
Confirm no cancellations went unfilled
Every empty slot is revenue that cannot be recovered. Front desk should have a same-day fill protocol — confirm it ran overnight.
Patient experience — ongoing through the day
Greet at least one patient by name in the waiting area
Small gesture. Outsized impact on loyalty and referrals. The older, higher-income patient you want to attract notices this most.
Ask one satisfied patient for a Google review today
In-person ask plus same-day text link is the highest-conversion approach. Keep it personal — not scripted. At 108 reviews, the goal is steady weekly adds, not a one-time push.
Confirm every patient left with a next appointment
Pre-schedule before checkout. The hygiene reappointment target is 90%+. One patient leaving without a scheduled return is one patient at risk of becoming inactive.
End of day — 3 min
Check actual vs. scheduled production for the day
Know the number. Daily patterns reveal scheduling gaps and case acceptance problems before they compound into a bad month.
Note any patient feedback — positive or negative
Surface to the team the next morning. Positive feedback should be celebrated. Negative patterns need to be caught within days, not months.

Weekly CEO checklist

Hannah + Dr. Mohammad — 30 min every Monday or Friday. Resets each session.

Revenue Production review
Review week's production vs. monthly target pace
Running total toward $100K monthly goal. Are you on pace, behind, or ahead? Adjust the following week's schedule if behind.
Pull and review the pending treatment report
Who has unscheduled treatment from the past 30 days? Assign follow-up calls. This report is where your hidden revenue lives.
Review collections vs. production this week
Collections should track at 98%+ of production. A gap here indicates billing or insurance processing issues that compound fast if ignored.
Operations Schedule health
Check next 2 weeks' schedule fill rate
Target 85–90% capacity. Persistent gaps are a scheduling strategy problem — not a sign you need more new patients.
Review last week's cancellations and no-shows
Track the rate week over week. Over 10% consistently is a red flag requiring a confirmation process overhaul and potentially a deposit policy.
Confirm hygiene reappointment rate for the week
Every patient leaving without a scheduled hygiene return is a leak. At 75–90 new patients per month, even a 15% leak compounds significantly over 12 months.
Marketing Online presence and growth
Check for new Google reviews and respond to each
Respond to every review — 5-star and otherwise. Response time and quality matter for search ranking and for the patients reading your responses before they book.
Confirm social posts went live this week
Minimum 2 posts per week. Is the content authentic to Atlas's voice, or does it feel like a template? Dr. Mohammad appearing on camera — even briefly — outperforms any graphic.
Any referral relationship follow-up needed this week?
Track outreach to specialists, physicians, and community partners. Professional relationships go cold without consistent, genuine contact — not just a flyer drop.
Check marketing vendor deliverables for the week
What was produced? What did it cost in time or money? Require data on new patient inquiries from digital channels — not just activity reports.
Team Culture and performance
Give one specific piece of positive feedback to a team member
Name the behavior, not just the outcome. "The way you explained that treatment plan to the patient in room 2 was exactly right" lands differently than "good job." Team retention is a revenue strategy.
Address any patient experience issues from the week
Resolve quickly. Close the loop with the patient if needed. A complaint handled well often produces a loyal patient. Ignored, it becomes a negative review.

Monthly CEO review

Hannah + Dr. Mohammad — 60 min at month end, before the next month begins. Resets each session.

Revenue Financial performance
Total production vs. monthly target
Where did you land? What drove the result — more patients, higher case value, or both? Understanding the why matters more than the number alone.
Production per patient visit
Target: $675+. March actual: $498 (median $250). If median stays below $300 by Q2, treatment planning and case presentation need direct intervention — training, scripting, or both. The goal is converting hygiene-only visits into scheduled restorative treatment.
Production breakdown: hygiene / restorative / elective
Elective services — aligners, whitening, cosmetic, night guards — should grow as a percentage each quarter. If it's flat, the patient mix isn't shifting toward your target demographic.
Insurance mix: what percentage is Medicaid this month?
Target: 25% or below. Track the trend. The mix shifts by growing other categories faster — not by dropping Medicaid patients. Is the trend moving in the right direction?
Collections rate this month
Target: 98%+ of production. If collections are running below 95% consistently, audit the billing process. This is a silent drain that compounds over 12 months.
Operations Practice health
New patient count and referral sources
75–90 is the current benchmark. Where are they coming from? Track by source: Google, referral, insurance directory, social, word of mouth. Double down on what's working.
Treatment acceptance rate this month
Target: 65%+. If below 55%, significant revenue is being presented and declined. This requires a case presentation conversation with the clinical team — not a marketing fix.
Hygiene reappointment rate
Target: 90%+. This is your retention rate in disguise. A growing practice with a poor reappointment rate is filling a leaky bucket.
Cancellation and no-show rate
Keep under 10%. If trending up, review the confirmation process. Chronic offenders may need a deposit policy — which is reasonable and defensible at a premium practice.
Schedule utilization rate
Target: 85–90% of available chair time filled. If consistently below 75%, the problem is scheduling strategy — not patient volume. If above 90%, evaluate whether a second hygienist is needed.
Marketing Growth levers
Google review count and rating
Currently 108 five-star reviews — a genuine asset. Track monthly adds. The goal is consistent weekly growth. Rating should never dip below 4.8. Respond to every review.
Marketing vendor accountability review
What did you spend? What did it return in new patient inquiries and booked appointments? Require specific data — not just activity reports. If they can't tell you cost per acquisition, that's the answer.
Social media: follower growth and engagement rate
Engagement rate matters more than follower count. Track which content types perform best. Is Dr. Mohammad showing up authentically? Social serves as validation, not primary lead generation.
Referral source tracking: who sent patients this month?
Thank referral sources — personally. A handwritten note or a lunch goes a long way. Double down on productive relationships. Let cold ones warm back up with a genuine touchpoint.
Team People and culture
Team pulse check: any retention risks?
Losing a hygienist or front desk lead mid-growth is a significant setback — both in production and patient experience. Stay ahead of it. Ask. Listen. Act before someone gives notice.
Training or development need identified?
Case presentation, patient communication, billing accuracy, aligner consultations. Invest in the team before problems surface as production gaps.
Share monthly production results with the full team
Teams that know the numbers perform better. Celebrate the wins specifically. Frame gaps as shared challenges with a shared solution. The team's engagement is a revenue strategy.
Strategic decision: the one move for next month
Identify the single highest-leverage action for the coming month
Not a list of ten things. One thing. What will move the production number or the patient mix most? Be specific about what success looks like in 30 days.
Assign clear ownership and a deadline to that action
If a priority has no named owner and no date, it will not happen. Decide here. Write it down. Check it at the next monthly review.
